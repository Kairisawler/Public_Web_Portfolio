My Name is Kai! But who am I really?

First and foremost, I'm a composer, programmer, web developer and a nerd! While my introduction on the home page may have given you an idea of who I am, it doesn't do much to explain who I am as a person, because that's what I am; a person! I've been working as a composer since early 2011; professionally from early 2013. Web Development (such as this site!) and Programming came much later, as I began that journey in the summer of 2017, when my application to the Nova Scotia Community College was accepted!

As a Composer...

Music has been a major part of my life, both daily and in general, for a very long time. When I was young I loved singing and whistling, and eventually I began piano lessons in my early teens. While piano lessons didn't last more than a couple years, music stuck with me as a need, rather than a want. I joined school bands from grade 6 onwards, choir for a number of years, and eventually found my love for composing in high school as part of an assignment in my music classes.

It began with piano, eventually going on to be small electronic pieces, designed for small JRPGs through the RPG-Maker community. As I worked in that community, small commissions here and there, eventually Degica (the company who created RPG-Maker) commissioned me for a music pack. Thanks to the success of that small music pack, I went on to create four additional packs for them and truly began my career as a professional composer.

While I continued to work on commissions, small indie-games and my own personal projects, I was eventually shown games by Paradox Interactive Studios AB, mainly Europa Universalis IV; a 'world conquest' style game, based in the mid 1400s to the early 1800s. I fell in love with the game, and eventually created a small music mod for the game, adding my own original music I created specifically for the game! As the mod grew and became popular, the lead developer for the game at the time, who went by Wiz, promoted my mod on their forums, and twitter; eventually getting in talks with me to commission me for a musical DLC for the game! It was also a great success, and lead to another two DLCs being commissioned in the following year (2016).

As a Programmer & Web Developer...

Summer 2017, that's when this officially began. But it wasn't just a knee-jerk decision to delve into the IT Industry and learn to program and develop websites; no, this goes back to my childhood. My father runs a software development company, here in Nova Scotia, and when I was just starting out as a composer, he hired me, mostly so I'd have some steady income, at the company. Initially, I was a support tech, someone to answer support phone-calls and e-mails, assist with minor code testing for the other programmers, as well as general testing for the mobile-based apps. I began to pick up minor bits of html, javascript and the occational python as the months went by. By the time I left to focus on my own composing business, I had had my interest in software development and web development peaked!

Finally, after working so long on video games for small and big developers alike, writing their music and creating the sound effects their games thrive on; I decided to make my own. I wanted to be able to have a project to work on in the slow months that I wouldn't need to prioritize during the busy months. The only problem was I didn't know how to code! Thus, my father suggested NSCC's IT Programming program, and I applied, was accepted, and eventually came to create this portfolio for. While this portfolio was originally just as a way to complete a project for a class, it's going to stick with me forever; constantly updated, improved upon and expanded.
